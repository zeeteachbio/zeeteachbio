1.1.1 Relationship of Biology with Other Sciences

Biology is a multidimensional subject and is linked with other sciences. The movement of animals follows the laws of motion in physics. Biology is considered an interdisciplinary science because it is related to many other scientific fields. Some important relationships are:

Biophysics

A branch of physics in which the laws and techniques of physics are applied to explain life processes. Radiophysics uses radioactive isotopes to trace the translocation of materials within organisms. Radio-labeling and carbon-dating determine the age of fossils. The use of sound waves in ultrasound and the use of lasers also show the connection between physics and biology.

Biomathematics / Biometry

A branch of mathematics that collects data about living organisms. It plays an important role in research.

Biochemistry

A branch of biology that deals with molecules forming living organisms or cells. It requires knowledge of both biology and chemistry to explain the synthesis of biomolecules and the functions of different molecules in the body.

Biogeography

Deals with the distribution of living organisms in different geographical regions of the world. Many organisms are restricted to specific regions due to environmental conditions.

Bio-economics

Deals with economically important organisms involved in production. It includes calculating cost value and profit value in processes such as meat production.
