1.3 Relationship of Biology with Other Sciences

1.3.1 relate biology to other branches of science, i.e., biophysics, biochemistry, biostatistics, biomathematics (biometry), biogeography, bio-economics, computational biology and biotechnology;

Biophysics

Definition: Biophysics applies the principles of physics to study biological structures and processes.
Relation to Biology: It helps explain movement, muscle contraction, vision, nerve impulses, and energy flow in living organisms.

Biochemistry

Definition: Biochemistry studies the chemical substances and reactions occurring inside living organisms.
Relation to Biology: It explains metabolism, enzymes, hormones, DNA, and how chemical changes control life processes.

Biostatistics

Definition: Biostatistics deals with the application of statistical methods to biological data.
Relation to Biology: It helps analyze experimental results, population studies, disease patterns, and scientific research data.

Biomathematics (Biometry)

Definition: Biomathematics uses mathematical models to understand biological systems.
Relation to Biology: It helps in predicting population growth, spread of diseases, genetics ratios, and ecosystem changes.

Biogeography

Definition: Biogeography studies the distribution of organisms across different regions of the world.
Relation to Biology: It explains why certain plants and animals live in specific habitats and how environment affects biodiversity.

Bio-economics

Definition: Bio-economics applies economic principles to the management of biological resources.
Relation to Biology: It helps in sustainable use of forests, fisheries, agriculture, livestock, and conservation of natural resources.

Computational Biology

Definition: Computational biology uses computer technology, algorithms, and software to study biological data.
Relation to Biology: It helps analyze DNA sequences, predict protein structure, track epidemics, and aid in modern research.

Biotechnology

Definition: Biotechnology uses living organisms or their products to make useful materials.
Relation to Biology: It supports medicine (vaccines, insulin), agriculture (GM crops), industry (enzymes), and environmental cleanup.
