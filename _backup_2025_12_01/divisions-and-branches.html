1.1 Divisions and Branches of Biology

With the increase in information in biology, it is divided into many divisions and branches.

1. Divisions of Biology

Biology has three major divisions:

(i) Zoology

Derived from Greek “Zoon” (animals) and “Logos” (study).

It deals with the study of animals.

(ii) Botany

Derived from Greek “Butane” (plants) and “Logos” (study).

It deals with the study of plants.

(iii) Microbiology

It deals with the study of microscopic organisms such as bacteria, which can be seen only with the help of a microscope.

2. Branches of Biology

Some important specialized branches of biology are:

(i) Morphology

Study of external form and structure of organisms.

(ii) Anatomy

Study of internal parts of living organisms by cutting them open.

(iii) Cell Biology

Study of the cell and its organelles.

(iv) Histology

Study of the structure of tissues of plants and animals.

(v) Physiology

Study of functions of living organisms.

(vi) Taxonomy

Study of rules and principles for grouping and naming living organisms.

(vii) Genetics

Study of heredity—transfer of characters from parents to offspring.

(viii) Developmental Biology

Study of the formation and development of the embryo.

(ix) Environmental Biology

Study of the relationship between living organisms and non-living environmental factors.

(x) Paleontology

Study of ancient life using fossils.

(xi) Biotechnology

Study of techniques for manipulating genes to bring desirable changes in gene structure or location.

(xii) Socio-biology

Study of social behavior and interactions among living organisms.

(xiii) Parasitology

Study of parasites.

(xiv) Pharmacology

Study of the action of drugs.

(xv) Molecular Biology

Study of organic molecules that make up the cell and its organelles.
