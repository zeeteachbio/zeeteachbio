Understanding Mitosis vs Meiosis

Published on November 20, 2025 • 5 min read

Cell division is a fundamental process in biology, essential for growth, repair, and reproduction. Two main types of cell division exist in eukaryotic cells: Mitosis and Meiosis. While they share some similarities, their outcomes and purposes are distinct.

What is Mitosis?

Mitosis is the process of cell division that results in two genetically identical daughter cells developing from a single parent cell. It is used for organic growth of tissues, fibers, and membranes.

  • Purpose: Growth and repair.
  • Outcome: Two diploid cells (2n).
  • Occurrence: Somatic (body) cells.

What is Meiosis?

Meiosis is a type of cell division that reduces the number of chromosomes in the parent cell by half and produces four gamete cells. This process is required to produce egg and sperm cells for sexual reproduction.

  • Purpose: Sexual reproduction.
  • Outcome: Four haploid cells (n).
  • Occurrence: Germ cells (gonads).

Key Differences

The main difference lies in the number of divisions. Mitosis involves one division, while meiosis involves two successive divisions. Furthermore, mitosis creates identical clones, whereas meiosis introduces genetic variation through crossing over.

Summary: Remember, Mitosis is for "My Toes" (body growth), and Meiosis is for "Me" (making a new organism).
